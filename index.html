About me

      Hello! My name is Olivier, and I am a mathematical modeling engineer and a master's graduate in the AI for Sciences program at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) in South Africa. Growing up in a family with limited financial means and in a village where agriculture is the main activity, I witnessed firsthand the challenges related to healthcare access and agriculture. Seasonal floods in my village not only disrupt daily life but also reduce the available farmland, presenting a significant climate challenge. Additionally, the education system in isolated areas like mine is often biased and unequal. These experiences fueled my desire to make a difference. To address these challenges, I focused on AI as a powerful tool that can effectively tackle issues in healthcare, climate, and education.

      My journey began with an Engineering degree in mathematical modeling in my home country, Benin, where I acquired essential knowledge in applying mathematics to real-world problems and honed my computer science skills. This foundation led to my receiving the Google DeepMind scholarship at AIMS in South Africa, where I pursued a master's degree in AI for Science. Through this enriching program, I gained deep expertise in Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and AI as a whole. Each step has brought me closer to my ultimate goal: to use AI to benefit my community. Achieving this goal will not only have a positive impact on my community but also on communities across Africa and around the world. I am also committed to the ethical use of AI and strive to uphold these principles in my work.
